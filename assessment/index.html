▶ How the final risk assessment works Method note

Risk is calculated from four evidence types using fixed weights, then adjusted by conservative interpretation rules. Each case receives three outputs: a risk score, a risk level, and a finding reliability label.

Weighted evidence components

Greater weight is assigned to more direct indicators. Semantic familiarity and repeated-output stability provide supporting context.

35%
Match with public benchmark text
30%
Near-verbatim recall
20%
Preference for known benchmark wording
15%
Unusually consistent repeated outputs
Additional interpretation rules

The weighted score is not used mechanically. Two conservative rules modify interpretation when evidence is especially strong or unusually narrow.

Direct evidence override
Cases with very strong benchmark overlap or near-verbatim recall cannot be assigned below High risk, even when other signals are weaker.
Single-signal caution
If elevated risk is driven only by repeated-output stability, without support from other evidence types, the final level is capped at Medium until corroborated.
How the score is interpreted
Low · 0–24
Medium · 25–49
High · 50–74
Critical · 75–100
How finding reliability is assigned
Reliability reflects how strongly the evidence signals support one another.
High — multiple strong signals converge, including direct evidence
Medium — at least two signals point in the same direction
Low — evidence is sparse, mixed, or mostly indirect
This is a structured risk assessment, not direct proof of contamination. It is designed to support cautious comparison and human review under black-box API constraints.
